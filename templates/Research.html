History and Status of Water Reservoirs in Beed District, Maharashtra, India

  • Beed district in Maharashtra, India, has a rich history of water reservoirs.
  • The Bindusara reservoir, constructed near the village Palli in 1955, serves as the primary drinking water supply for Beed city.
  • Another significant reservoir is the Jawla reservoir, built in 1995 on 441 hectares of land in Kaij Taluka.
  • It initially served agricultural purposes but now also provides drinking water to neighboring settlements.
  • The Jayakwadi Stage II Dam, opened in 1987, is another crucial water reservoir in the city.

Factors and Causes Affecting Water Reservoirs

  1. Climate Change and Rainfall: Beed district has been a victim of the El Nino effect from 2010 to 2017, causing uneven rainfall patterns and droughts.
  2. Industrialization and Urbanization: The effects of industrialization, urbanization, and the careless use of pesticides have strained the water quality of reservoirs like the Jawla Reservoir.
  3. Overuse of Aquifers: The overuse of aquifers and their slow recharge have led to water scarcity.
  4. Wastewater and Natural Disasters: Wastewater, natural disasters, and increased human consumption contribute to water scarcity.

Effects of Water Reservoirs

  1. Water Quality: The water quality of reservoirs like the Jawla Reservoir has been under strain due to runoff from industrialization, urbanization, and pesticide use.
  2. Water Scarcity: Overuse of aquifers, increased human consumption, and wastewater have led to water scarcity.
  3. Flooding: The discharge of water from dams like the Manjara dam has led to flooding in villages under Kaij and Ambajogai talukas of Beed district.
  4. Pollution: Industrial wastes, sewage disposal, deforestation, and excessive use of fertilizers and pesticides have threatened the water quality of reservoirs like the Sindphana River.

Several initiatives are underway to improve water management.

      The government and other organizations are implementing various initiatives to improve water management in Beed district.
      These initiatives include rainwater harvesting, watershed development, and promoting water conservation practices.
